What is the principle of waste disposal?

The recovery principle is based on the thermochemical conversiontechnology.

Thermochemical conversion is a chain thermochemical reaction of decomposition and conversion of carbon-containing materials (wastes) into synthesis gas (syngas), which produces a considerable amount of thermal energy (when an additional module is installed on the Utilizator module the thermal energy may be converted into electricity).

Utilizator module, model D-500

What is the recycling process?

The reactor of the mobile facility (see diagram) takes roughly 30 minutes to heat up to 400 °С by burning conventional charcoal, which is enough to start a thermochemical conversion chain reaction.

The carbon-containing waste is loaded into the reactor, which has a limited access of oxygen. In the reactor, as a result of the thermochemical decomposition process the waste is converted into high calorific synthesis gas, which is fed into the vortex combustor (see diagram) and is used to generate thermal energy.

The operating temperature range in the reactor is 1100 °С to 2000 °С, with the maximum temperature of gases leaving the system via the exhaust system (see diagram) not more than 265 °С. The ash residue has potential commercial applications (construction, petrochemistry).

The key difference between the thermochemical conversion method and burning is the lack of oxygen. Therefore, there are no conditions for the generation of toxic and poisonous compounds (dioxins, furanes, etc.). The level of emissions into the atmosphere is lower than that of a typical gas-fired boiler-houses.

Thus, this waste disposal technology is the most environmentally friendly.
